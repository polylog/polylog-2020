Projects

Qatar 2022: launch of the public relations campaign

In 2022 Qatar will host 2022 FIFA World Cup™. The main task of the project was to make pleasant and unforgettable acquaintance of fans with the country during The World Cup in Russia. The organizer of interactive objects for Muscovites and guests of the capital was the Ceremonies Staging Agency. Complex information support of the project was performed by Polylog Consulting Group.

In 2022 Qatar will host 2022 FIFA World Cup™. The main task of the project was to make pleasant and unforgettable acquaintance of fans with the country during The World Cup in Russia. The organizer of interactive objects for Muscovites and guests of the capital was the Ceremonies Staging Agency. Complex information support of the project was performed by Polylog Consulting Group.

Bloggers and journalists

For the first time The World Cup will be held in Qatar, for the first time — in the middle East, for the first time — in winter. We were convinced of the absolute validity of the decision in May when we have been conducting a blog tour and a press tour to the capital of Qatar, Doha. The program of the tours included: visiting The World Cup stadiums, cultural and infrastructure facilities. It was so hot that most of the streets were deserted before sunset. After the snowy Moscow spring, it was literally a test for us.

The blog tour have been conducted from 10 to 13 May along with pranker Vitaly Zdorovetchi, extreme-rufer Rascalov Vitaly and Sergey Alexeev, the author of the blog “Real football”.

The press tour have been held from 11 to 14 May. 19 journalists from 17 publishing houses arrived in Qatar: Match TV, Russia Today, Championat.com, Sports.ru, TASS, RIA Novosti, Rossiyskaya Gazeta, Interfax and others.

Back to Moscow

Working on the project, it was important for us not only to talk about where and how the next championship will be held, but also to overcome the stereotype of Qatar as a closed state. We have been striving to bring the message that Qatar is safe, modern and progressive country with a huge tourism and investment potential.

The Ceremonies Staging Agency developed offline activities to show Qatar for Muscovites and guests of the capital, thanks to which everyone felt themselves as a part of the upcoming grand event. The CSA have created:

Qatar-Russia Portals

Placed in iconic locations in Moscow, St Petersburg and Doha, the interactive portals connected fans from around the world and gave them a preview of the plans for 2022. The Portals destroyed the language barrier between fans from the whole world.

Qatar @roadto2022 Exhibition

The collection of rare FIFA World Cup™ memorabilia at GUM in Red Square, which also included models of the stadiums being constructed for The 2022 Tournament. At the end of the journey through the past, present and future of The World Cup, visitors recorded their messages to qatari people, thereby playing a role in the history of football.

Majlis Qatar

Located in Moscow’s Gorky Park, Majlis Qatar introduced Qatar and the 2022 FIFA World Cup™. Fans became immersed in qatari culture while enjoying live matches and learning more about the opportunities available in Qatar. Also there have been sculptures in the form of Arab Oryx — the national animal of Qatar. The sculptures were created specifically for the exhibition by famous artists of Russia and Qatar.

Qatar Elements

It is the world’s first temporary digital museum on the water. This unique LED light show on the Moskva river served as a beacon to visitors near and far. It brought Qatar’s natural and manmade wonders to life for thousands of fans.

Results

At the beginning of 2019, 48 metro stations will be opened in Doha and an updated system of public and water transport will be launched. They will connect five stadiums of The World Championship. But for us the Arabic tale has come to an end and it is time to take stock.

From 6 to 15 July more than 750 000 people visited “Qatar @roadto2022 Exhibition” at GUM. There were 11 TV stories, 4 printed materials, 273 publications in online editions.
